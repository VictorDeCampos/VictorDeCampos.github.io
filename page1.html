About Me

Wait... Ducks can do VFX?

How did we get here?

Since I was a kid I've had a passion for game development. I've explored many of the fields that make up videogames and developed both artistic and technical abilities over time. First using tutorials on the internet, books and workshops, and finally choosing it as my career in college.

I decided to do a double degree (at least that's how it's called here in Spain) so I'd have both an Animation degree and a Game Design degree at the end of it. I love both worlds, and I've learned many of the abilities needed in both the game and animation pipeline:

Storyboarding, Illustration, Concept-art, 3D Modeling, Texturing, Rendering, Sculpting, Rigging, 3D & 2D Animation, Video Editing, Sound Design, Narrative Design, C# Programming, Multiplayer Environments...

And many many more classes that I passed with extraordinary grades (More than 20 honorable mentions)
I also developed teamworking skills, I learned the importance of giving and receiving feedback and I even worked as Project Leader on one of our college's animation projects.

Together with a friend of mine, we brought back the Videogame Design Club on our University and were able to get docens of people together with our same passions. We also organized gamejams with up to a hundred atendees, and I learned to coordinate a team and manage events as best as I could throughout the four years I was president.

Last gamejam I organized (+100 participants) in 2024

Videogame Design Club (UEGameStudio)

I've not only organized but also participated on several gamejams during these years, and I plan to continue doing it, as I learn so much from them! I was able to get 20th place on a Brackleys Gamejam and I even won some of the Gamejams from my college (when I wasn't part of the organization of course haha)

Throughout my third and fourth year of college I understood that, despite how much I'd like it, I couldn't do every single step of a production at once. Thus began my journey to find what to specialize on. After more than a year going back and forwards with my thoughts, I finally stumbled upon Game VFXs: A field where my artistic and creative nature could be combined with the technical aspects of game development.

After some more thinking I finally decided to go all-in with it and started learning on VFX Apprentice.

I've since made several effects, with diferent camera perspectives, colors, textures and styles. I was able to use my knowledge of the fundamentals from both my degrees, while also learning many new techniques such as Flipbook-making, Shader Creation, Particle Systems, Dissolves, Vertex Offset Shaders and many many more.

Thanks to all of this my portfolio, my knowledge and my passion are growing every day, as I discover new techniques, get feedback from professionals, and find new effects to use as reference and learn from.
